Discover The Amazing World Of clams!

Classification

  • Kingdom: Animals
  • Phylum: Mollusca
  • Class: Bivalvia
  • Order: Veneroida

Anatomy

  • Shell: Two hinged shells that protect the soft body inside.
  • Body: Soft body inside the shells, including the foot for movement.
  • Muscle: A large adductor muscle that holds the shells together.
  • Mantle: Soft tissue that surrounds the body and secretes the shell.
  • Gills: Used for filtering food and oxygen from water.

Dietary Behavior

Clams are filter feeders, meaning they eat by filtering small particles from the water, such as:

  • Plankton
  • Algae
  • Organic detritus

They use their gills to filter water and capture food particles.

Life Span

Clams can live for many years. Some species can live for over 100 years, depending on the type of clam!

Toxicity

Most clams are safe to eat, but some species may contain harmful toxins like red tide, which can cause illness if consumed.

Natural Habitat

Clams live in a variety of environments, including:

  • Shallow waters
  • Deep underwater areas
  • Sand or mud substrates

They are commonly found in coastal areas and can burrow into the sand or mud.
