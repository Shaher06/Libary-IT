Discover The Amazing World Of snails!

Classification

  • Kingdom: Animals
  • Phylum: Mollusca
  • Class: Gastropoda
  • Order: Stylommatophora
  • Shell: Coiled shell that provides protection and is made of calcium carbonate.
  • Body: Soft, slimy body with a foot used for movement.
  • Eyes: Tentacles on top of the head with eyes at the tips.
  • Mouth: Located on the underside of the body, with a radula for scraping food.
  • Breathing: Some snails breathe through gills, while others breathe through a lung-like structure.

Dietary Behavior

Snails are herbivores (plant eaters) and feed on:

  • Leaves
  • Algae
  • Fungi

They use their radula (a tongue-like organ) to scrape off bits of food.

Life Span

Snails typically live 2 to 5 years, although some species can live much longer in the right conditions.

Toxicity

Some snails, such as the cone snail, are venomous and can pose a danger to humans. However, most snails are harmless.

Natural Habitat

Snails are found in a variety of habitats, including:

  • Moist environments like gardens, forests, and ponds
  • Marine environments (sea snails)
  • Freshwater lakes and rivers

They prefer humid, moist conditions and often hide in damp areas during the day.
